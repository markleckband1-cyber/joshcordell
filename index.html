The coach who walks with your kid.

Josh Cordell grew up in Bend, Oregon — and never really left. Not because he couldn't, but because he found something here worth staying for: the teenagers who needed someone to show up for them, again and again, without an agenda.

The Coaching Years

For 17 years, Josh served as a varsity head tennis coach at the high school level in Central Oregon — 21 seasons of coaching high school sports overall. In that span, his teams won eight Oregon Class 5A State team championships in a 10-season stretch, one of the most dominant runs in the history of the program. In 2014, he was named a finalist for National Coach of the Year.

But Josh will tell you the championships aren't the thing he's proudest of. His proudest moment came when a group of graduating seniors — after the state tournament had already ended and the season was officially over — asked to come back for one last practice. Not for trophies. Just to be together, one more time, with their coach.

That moment tells you everything about what Josh actually built on those courts. Not just champions. People.

The Walks

Somewhere in the middle of his coaching career, Josh started going on walks. One-on-one. No playbook, no clipboard. Just a teenager and a loop around the neighborhood. Over the years, those walks became the most important thing he did — more impactful, in many ways, than any drill or championship practice.

He's taken more than 5,000 of them. And what he learned on those walks became the foundation of everything he does today: the conviction that presence — real, patient, unglamorous presence — is the most powerful thing an adult can offer a young person.

Those 5,000 walks also became a book. With Changes Everything: What 5,000 Walks with Teens Taught Me About Trust, Connection and Presence was published in late 2025 and has already earned a 4.96 rating on Goodreads — remarkable for a first-time author. Readers describe it as less a how-to guide and more an invitation: to slow down, put away the fixes, and simply be with the young people in their lives.

Prep Success Coach & SKO Youth Sports

In 2016, Josh founded Prep Success Coach — a private youth mentoring and coaching practice built on the same method that produced those 5,000 walks. Working one-on-one and in small groups, Josh helps teenagers navigate the pressures of school, sports, identity, and adolescence. His clients' parents describe him as the adult their kid actually talks to.

Josh is also the Executive Director of SKO Youth Sports, a nonprofit organization based in Bend that works to champion a healthy, positive sports culture for all young athletes — especially those who can't afford private coaching. SKO serves as the community arm of Josh's mission: making sure that what he's learned about youth development reaches kids regardless of their family's income.

Westside Church & Community Life

Today, Josh serves as Youth and Family Pastor at Westside Church in Bend — a role that puts him at the center of hundreds of family relationships across Central Oregon. He leads youth programming, oversees men's ministry, and hosts events like Things Kids Are Telling Me Right Now, a community talk that draws parents, coaches, and educators who want to understand what today's teenagers are actually experiencing.

He's been featured in Bend Magazine, covered by The Source Weekly, and his content has been shared by the Changing the Game Project — one of the most respected organizations in youth sports. Josh also ran Central Oregon Athlete, a local sports media outlet, and has spoken alongside coaches, pastors, and youth development leaders at events across the region.

The Person Behind the Platform

Josh went to Mountain View High School in Bend. He's married to Emily Cordell and is a proud father. He loves burgers, guitar, golf, and — naturally — long walks. He is the kind of person who is genuinely, unhurriedly interested in whoever he's talking to. That quality, more than any credential, is what makes him unusually good at what he does.

He is not a therapist. He's not a guru. He's a mentor — someone who shows up, walks alongside, and believes that being truly present with a young person is one of the most countercultural and important things an adult can do in 2026.

"I've walked the same neighborhood loop more than 5,000 times — always with a teenager beside me. Over the years, I've discovered a simple truth: being with someone changes everything."

— Josh Cordell, With Changes Everything
